Methodology

Six-lens scoring (1 = low risk, 5 = high risk)
LensHow it is scored
CommodityComputed: Σ (cost-base weight × 0.4 supply concentration + 0.4 India import share + 0.2 price volatility). Weights from annual reports where disclosed, otherwise labeled estimates; concentration and import shares from USGS, PPAC, IEA, USDA, ICCO, Ministry of Steel.
PolicyComputed from the latest weekly cycle's impact status (urgent 5, material 4, watch 3, none 1). Official documents only.
CurrencyQualitative rating from disclosed FX structures: foreign-currency debt, royalty flows, revenue/cost currency mismatch.
FinancialQualitative rating from disclosed balance-sheet posture (net cash vs capex leverage vs refinancing dependence).
ConcentrationQualitative rating: product, customer, geography, or single-segment profit dependence per segment disclosures.
TransitionQualitative rating: decarbonization exposure, technology shifts, documented competitive disruption.
Data pipeline

A six-agent weekly cycle (planner → document scout → risk analyst → company mapper → rival monitor → service mapper) scans EUR-Lex, European Commission DGs, Consilium, RBI, DGFT, CBIC, PIB, WTO, USTR, Federal Register, and exchange filings. Reasoning is recorded before conclusions in each cycle JSON. Secondary sources are tagged and queued for primary retrieval. Live market data: open.er-api.com / frankfurter.dev (ECB) / gold-api.com, fetched client-side on open with an embedded dated snapshot as fallback.

Caveats
  • Company cost-share weights are estimates where issuers do not disclose input-cost breakdowns; anchored figures cite their filing.
  • Qualitative lens scores are structured judgments from public facts — a prioritization layer, not measurements.
  • Scenario mathematics assume 50% cost pass-through per processing step, no hedging or inventories — structural exposure, not price forecasts.
